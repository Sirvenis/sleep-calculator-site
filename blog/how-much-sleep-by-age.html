How Much Sleep Do You Need by Age? Complete Guide

Published: April 2026 | Reading time: 7 min

Sleep needs change dramatically throughout life. A newborn needs up to 17 hours daily, while most adults function well on 7-9 hours. Let's explore science-backed recommendations for every age group.

Signs You're Not Getting Enough Sleep

  • Needing an alarm clock to wake up (you should wake naturally near your alarm time)
  • Feeling drowsy during meetings, reading, or watching TV
  • Needing caffeine to function in the afternoon
  • Getting irritable over minor issues
  • Trouble concentrating or making decisions
  • Getting sick more often than usual

Quality vs. Quantity

Eight hours of fragmented sleep can leave you feeling worse than 6.5 hours of uninterrupted deep sleep. Room temperature (65-68°F), noise levels, light exposure, and stress all affect quality. Using a sleep calculator to align bedtime with 90-minute cycles maximizes restorative value.

Conclusion

While age-based guidelines provide a starting point, individual needs vary based on genetics, activity level, and health. Listen to your body, prioritize consistency, and optimize your sleep timing.
