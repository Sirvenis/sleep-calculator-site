10 Sleep Hygiene Tips Backed by Science

Published: April 2026 | Reading time: 7 min

Sleep hygiene refers to habits and environmental factors affecting sleep quality. Poor sleep hygiene is one of the most common reasons people struggle to fall asleep or wake unrefreshed. Here are 10 evidence-based tips.

1. Maintain a Consistent Schedule

Your circadian rhythm thrives on consistency. Going to bed and waking at the same time daily — including weekends — is the single most impactful change. Even 1-hour differences between weekday and weekend sleep ("social jetlag") affect health.

2. Optimize Bedroom Temperature

Your core body temperature must drop 2-3°F to initiate sleep. The ideal bedroom temperature is 65-68°F (18-20°C). A warm room prevents natural cooling, leading to more awakenings and less deep sleep.

3. Eliminate Light Pollution

Even small amounts of light suppress melatonin production. Use blackout curtains or a sleep mask. Cover LED indicators. If you need a nightlight, use red or amber wavelengths which have the least melatonin impact.

4. Create a Technology Curfew

Blue light from screens suppresses melatonin for up to 3 hours. Stop screen use 1 hour before bed. If you must use devices, enable night mode and reduce brightness. Replace screen time with reading a physical book or gentle stretching.

5. Watch Caffeine Intake

Caffeine has a 5-6 hour half-life. Half your afternoon coffee is still in your system at bedtime. Even if you can fall asleep, caffeine reduces deep sleep by up to 20%. Set a 2 PM caffeine cutoff.

6. Be Strategic About Alcohol

Alcohol helps you fall asleep but severely disrupts quality. It suppresses REM sleep, increases awakenings, and worsens sleep apnea. Finish your last drink at least 3 hours before bed.

7. Exercise Regularly — But Time It Right

Regular exercise improves sleep quality and reduces sleep onset time. However, intense exercise within 2-3 hours of bedtime raises core temperature and adrenaline. Morning or afternoon exercise is ideal.

8. Reserve Your Bed for Sleep

Your brain forms location-activity associations. If you work, eat, or scroll in bed, your brain stops associating it with sleep. This stimulus control therapy is one of the most effective insomnia treatments.

9. Manage Racing Thoughts

Anxiety and rumination are leading insomnia causes. Try the "worry journal" technique: spend 5 minutes writing down everything on your mind before bed. Studies show this reduces sleep onset time by 50%.

10. Use a Sleep Calculator

Timing sleep to complete full 90-minute cycles improves how rested you feel. Use a sleep calculator to work backwards from your wake-up time and find the optimal bedtime.

Building Your Routine

Don't implement all 10 tips at once. Start with 2-3 addressing your biggest challenges. Give each change 1-2 weeks before adding another. Consistency beats perfection.
